# ODIQ-ZnSe-Optically-Addressable-Deterministic-Ion-Implanted-Qubits-in-ZnSe
Investigating ion-implanted donor centres in zinc selenide (ZnSe) as a scalable, optically-addressable qubit platform — an alternative to NV centres in diamond, which suffer from low creation yield and poor spatial control.


Goal: Screen candidate donor species implanted into ZnSe wafers to identify optically-addressable spin defects suitable for quantum sensing / quantum information applications.

Why ZnSe: NV-centre-in-diamond platforms are limited by inefficient defect creation yield and implantation-depth control. ZnSe donor centres offer a potentially higher-yield, more controllable alternative.

Species        Group                Role
N               VII(halogen-like)   Donor candidate
Ga              III                 Donor candidate
In              III                 Donor candidate 
Kr              Noble gas           Control / comparison
